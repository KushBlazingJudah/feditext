What is Feditext, anyway?

Feditext is a federated textboard, similar in idea to FChannel.

Users can create text-only posts which will be propagated to other instances that follow a board on this instances. Users on other boards that we follow will be propagated to us. This allows a model where no one entity owns 100% of the network; anyone is free to spin up an instance of their own and join the network or to create their own network.

The source code is available on GitHub. Contributions, complaints, and bug reports are welcome.

What can I do while posting?

Post contents are limited to {{.postmax}} characters, however in that you can quote and cite other people and instances.

You can quote with a single ">"; the rest of the line will be marked as a post.

Example: abc >hello world -> abc >hello world

You can cite with ">>" and a post number, or like on FChannel, if you have an ActivityPub link you can cite with that too.

Example: >>22, >>https://fchan.xyz/prog/AF085BFA

The name field accepts any name {{.namelen}} long; this includes a marker that can be used as a unique identifier called a "tripcode". While entering your name, enter a single "#" for an insecure tripcode, enter "##" for a secure tripcode. Data after the hashes is called a "password"; do not use your actual password here. Your tripcode is generated from this password, and as such changes every time you change it.

The differences between an insecure tripcode and a secure tripcode is slim; one is much easier to predict and is commonly used for vanity, the other is much harder to predict as it uses secret data on this server.

The options field allows you to "noko", or return to a thread when you post, and "sage" doesn't bump the thread you post to. You can use "nokosage" to do both at once.

How do I view an entire thread?

Click the "#" on any post; it will bring you to the thread.

I keep seeing things like [5:2] on the top of threads. What do the numbers mean?

The first number is the amount of posts, the second number is the amount of unique posters or instances.

Why do the posts have sequential ID numbers?

Sequential ID numbers have not run their course, random base36 is not better.

On the ActivityPub side we actually use random hex IDs; this exists solely as a nod to FChannel's FAQ which has served as inspiration for most of this. These hex IDs are currently hidden behind the sequential ID system; they may be made accessible from your side eventually.
